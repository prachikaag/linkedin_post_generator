The era of one AI tool for everything is quietly ending. 🌱

What's replacing it is more interesting. Clio, a legal tech platform, this week crossed $500M in annual revenue — built entirely around AI that understands legal workflows, not just text. One industry. Real depth. Real scale.

The same pattern is appearing across sectors. 🔍
Purpose-built AI agents for financial services launched recently. An AI system is now running across scientific research and infrastructure in the background — not answering prompts, running processes. This week, a $200 million partnership pointed AI at global health and development. These aren't horizontal tools with a new coat of paint.

They're built around the specific decisions, risks, and language of one field. 🏗️
The gap between general AI and purpose-built AI — in terms of what actually gets done — is becoming hard to ignore.

The horizontal layer isn't going away. But the value is moving to depth. 💡
The tools winning right now are the ones that understand your industry's compliance requirements, your customer's language, your team's actual workflow. A workplace AI assistant recently reported users saving between two and twenty hours a week. That range only happens when the tool fits the context.

For teams building serious workflows: general AI got everyone started. ✨
Vertical AI is what gets you ahead.
