Most marketing teams are still using AI like a fancy search engine.

That's the gap. And it's about to close fast.

In the last 90 days, OpenAI shipped Operator, Anthropic shipped tool-use and computer use, and Google launched Agentic Search. Every major AI lab is now pointing at the same thing: AI that doesn't just *answer* questions — it *does* tasks.

I spent two weeks building a workflow that monitors 20+ AI news feeds, scores articles by how relevant they are to my topics, checks what's trending on Google, and drafts LinkedIn posts in my voice — all before I've had my coffee. My job is now to edit and decide what ships, not to hunt for content and stare at a blank page.

The output isn't perfect. But it's 80% there, and that's the point.

Here's what I think brands are missing: the shift isn't from "no AI" to "AI writes our stuff." It's from "AI as a tool you open" to "AI as a workflow that runs in the background." That's a completely different operating model.

Brands that figure out agentic workflows in 2025 will have a structural advantage — not because AI replaces their team, but because one person with the right setup will be able to do what used to take four.
