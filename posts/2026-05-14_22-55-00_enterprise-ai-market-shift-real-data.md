Something shifted in enterprise AI this week. It's bigger than any one announcement. 🌊

The pattern isn't one company doing well. It's the whole market committing. Real spend data from Ramp — tracking actual business payments — shows companies paying for AI at a scale that didn't exist a year ago. The gap between platforms is narrow. The direction of travel isn't.

PwC is retraining tens of thousands of staff on AI this week. 🏁
Not a pilot. The core of how they deliver work to clients. That's a business model bet, not an experiment.

And it's not just the top end of the market. 🤝
Tools that were enterprise-only a year ago launched small business versions this week. Both ends of the market, same product cycle. That compression is genuinely new.

Microsoft recently crossed 20 million paying users on its AI assistant. An AI hardware company went public on Nasdaq this week and had a remarkable first day. 📈
The public markets are now open to this space — not just venture capital.

The story isn't about who's winning. It's about a whole category growing up at once. ✨
Enterprises committing. Small businesses getting access. Infrastructure going public.

That's not hype. That's a market finding its footing.
